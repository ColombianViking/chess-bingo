Glossary & Rules
  • Piece = Bishop, Knight, Rook, Queen
  • The .. dots denote any opponent move. For example: 1. e4 .. 2. Nf3 show that no matter what blacks move is, Nf3 is to be played afterwards.
  1. Each square completed is worth as much as it's difficulty. The difficulty is shown as green, yellow or red for 1, 2 or 3 points respectively
  2. Each row completed is worth as much as the sum of its squares difficulty, divided by 5, rounded up.
  3. A tie in the bingo competition will be decided by the placing in the normal tournament.
  4. Bring your bingo card to your match. Use a small dot to indicate if you think you've completed a square. If you feel unsure, pause the clock on your turn and verify with the enemy team.
  5. After the game, fill in or cross out the squares you have completed.
  6. Squares won't be awarded retroactively, these have to be decided while both teams are at the board.
  7. Have some manners. Achievement hunting when you are in a totally winning position is very rude.
